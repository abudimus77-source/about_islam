Before You Judge Islam

This page isn’t here to argue with you.
It’s here to offer clarity.

What is Islam?

Islam is belief in one God and living with moral responsibility, justice, and compassion. Muslims believe in the same God worshipped by Abraham, Moses, and Jesus.

“We have not sent you, ˹O Muhammad,˺ except as a mercy for all people.” Qur’an 21:107

“Is Islam violent?”

Violence against innocent people is strictly forbidden in Islam. Acts of terror contradict its core teachings.

“Whoever kills an innocent person, it is as if they have killed all of humanity.” Qur’an 5:32

“What does Jihad really mean?”

Jihad literally means struggle. The primary struggle in Islam is personal: to be honest, just, and morally disciplined.

“And strive for God with the striving He deserves.” Qur’an 22:78

“Does Islam force belief?”

Faith in Islam must be a free and conscious choice. Forced conversion is explicitly forbidden.

“There is no compulsion in religion.” Qur’an 2:256

“How does Islam view women?”

Islam granted women legal rights to property, inheritance, education, and consent in marriage over 1,400 years ago.

“Indeed, the most honored of you in the sight of God is the most righteous among you.” Qur’an 49:13
